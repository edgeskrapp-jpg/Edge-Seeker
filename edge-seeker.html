📖 HOW TO READ THIS
GRADE SYSTEM
Grade A — Highest Confidence
Multiple independent data sources agree. Optimal game window. Confirmed pitcher matchup. Edge 8%+ with 70+ model confidence.
Grade B — Solid Discrepancy
Most signals point the same direction. Some data still pending confirmation. Edge 5–8% with 55+ confidence.
Grade C — Early Look
Limited data available. Check back closer to game time for updated analysis. Edge 3–5% with 40+ confidence.
KEY TERMS
Edge %
The difference between our model's calculated true probability and the market's implied probability. A positive edge means our model calculates this team as more likely to win than the market implies.
Kelly %
A mathematical formula suggesting proportional allocation based on edge size and odds. For reference only — not a recommendation of any kind.
True Win Prob
Our model's calculated probability of this team winning, based on all available data including Poisson distributions, park factors, and Elo ratings.
Book Implied Prob
The probability implied by the current market odds, after removing the market's margin.
Elo Rating
A dynamic team strength rating updated after every game result. Higher rating indicates stronger recent performance relative to the rest of the league.
Moneyline
The market odds for each team to win outright. Our model calculates a true probability and compares it to what the odds imply.
+ odds (e.g. +140)
The market implies this team has less than a 50% probability of winning. Our model may calculate a higher probability, creating a potential discrepancy.
− odds (e.g. −160)
The market implies this team has greater than a 50% probability of winning. Our model may calculate a lower probability, or confirm the favorite.
Over/Under
The projected total runs scored in the game. Our model calculates an expected run total using Poisson distributions and compares to the market line.
